# Workflows Foundation

Owner: Platform Architect
Last Updated: 2026-03-18
Version: 1.0
Status: Approved

---

## 1. Purpose

Define the official business workflow model for the Musahama platform.

This document is the source of truth for:
- how work moves through the system
- how modules relate to workflow stages
- how workflow tables differ from canonical data tables
- where workflow actions begin and end

---

## 2. Core Rule

The product is not a set of disconnected pages.

It is a connected workflow:

1. CRM opportunity enters the system
2. opportunity becomes a mandate
3. mandate produces research
4. research produces strategies and results
5. results are reviewed and acted on
6. final company/contact and analysis data is stored in canonical tables
7. exports, shortlist actions, and downstream actions occur from results

---

## 3. Official Workflow Path

### Step 1 — CRM Opportunities
Incoming opportunity or manually created business opportunity.

### Step 2 — Mandates
A defined search/targeting mandate based on the opportunity or manual input.

### Step 3 — Research
A research execution run attached to a mandate.

### Step 4 — Strategies
Structured research approaches generated or authored inside research.

### Step 5 — Results
Candidate outputs generated by research and strategies.

### Step 6 — Canonical Promotion
Resolved company/contact/entity data is stored in canonical tables.

### Step 7 — Analysis Output
Evidence, psych profile, fit analysis, and dossier are stored in normalized analysis tables.

---

## 4. Workflow Modules vs Data Layers

### Workflow modules
- crm_opportunities
- mandates
- research
- results

These drive process and execution.

### Canonical archive modules
- companies
- contacts

These are the final structured archives.

### Supporting modules
- integrations
- billing
- settings

These support execution but are not part of the core business workflow.

---

## 5. Workflow vs Source of Truth

### Workflow tables
Used for:
- process tracking
- execution status
- in-progress work
- lifecycle orchestration

Examples:
- crm_opportunities
- mandates
- research
- strategies
- research_results
- research_constraints

### Canonical tables
Used for:
- final company records
- final contact records
- final normalized analysis outputs

Examples:
- companies
- company_domains
- contacts
- contact_emails
- contact_phones
- evidence
- psych_profiles
- lmc_fits
- dossiers

---

## 6. Hard Rules

1. workflow tables are not automatically the final source of truth
2. results are a review/action module, not the final canonical archive
3. companies and contacts must not be treated as temporary result blobs
4. strategies are a subview of research, not a top-level module
5. shortlist and dossier are result actions/subviews, not top-level modules
6. workflow execution must remain organization-scoped

---

## 7. Organization Rule

Every workflow record is organization-owned.

Required ownership pattern where applicable:
- organization_id
- created_by_user_id
- updated_by_user_id

No workflow action may cross organization boundaries.

---

## 8. Runtime Capability Rule

Workflow actions are still governed by:
- auth/session validity
- current organization context
- module visibility rules
- feature availability rules
- provider/integration readiness where needed

Workflow documentation does not replace auth, authorization, or feature-system rules.
